# 📦 Submission Guide — AI Story Buddy & Quiz

Everything you need to record, profile, and submit a top-tier entry.

---

## 1. Demo Video Script (≈60–90s)

> Keep it tight. Show the happy path, one wrong answer, and one error recovery.

**0:00 — Hook (5s)**
> "This is *AI Story Buddy & Quiz* — a feature I built for Peblo. Meet Pip."
- Show launch: Pip bobbing, story card faded in.

**0:08 — Narration (15s)**
> "I tap Read Me A Story. Pip goes into a loading state with a friendly message…"
- Tap CTA → loading spinner + "Getting Pip ready…".
- "…then he narrates the story out loud with text-to-speech." (let a few words play)

**0:28 — Quiz reveal (8s)**
> "The quiz only appears *after* narration finishes — it's gated on the TTS
> completion callback. Watch it slide up."

**0:38 — Wrong answer (10s)**
> "If I pick the wrong answer, the card shakes, there's haptic feedback, and Pip
> gently encourages another try. The quiz stays put — no dead-ends for a 6-year-old."

**0:50 — Correct answer (12s)**
> "Pick the right one — confetti, a success card, and Pip gets visibly happier."

**1:05 — Error recovery (10s)**
> "And it's resilient: if TTS fails, the child sees a friendly retry card instead
> of a crash." (Optional: simulate by toggling airplane/disabling TTS.)

**1:15 — Close (10s)**
> "Built with Flutter, Riverpod, and Material 3. The quiz is fully JSON-driven —
> any number of options renders with no code changes. Thanks for watching!"

**Recording tips:** record on a real device with audio; turn on a screen-recorder
that captures device sound; keep your finger taps visible (enable *Show taps* in
Android developer options).

## 3. Flutter DevTools Profiling Steps

1. Run in **profile mode** (release-like, with tooling):
   ```bash
   flutter run --profile
   ```
2. Open **DevTools** (the link prints in the console, or via your IDE).
3. **Performance tab** → enable *Track Widget Builds* and *Performance Overlay*.
   - Tap through the flow. Confirm frames stay under the 16ms (60 FPS) line.
   - The two graphs (UI + Raster) should stay green during the buddy animation
     and confetti.
4. **CPU profiler** → record while confetti plays; confirm no single method
   dominates.
5. **Memory tab** → watch heap while re-running the story several times; confirm
   it returns to baseline (no leak — controllers are disposed).
6. **Rebuild counts** → tapping a wrong answer should rebuild the quiz card, not
   the story card or buddy (thanks to `.select()` + `RepaintBoundary`). Note this
   in your write-up — it's exactly what reviewers look for.

**What to capture:** a screenshot of the Performance overlay showing green bars,
and the rebuild stats showing isolated rebuilds.

## 5. How to Rank in the Top 10%

- **Lead with the gate.** The single most impressive correctness detail is that
  the quiz is tied to the TTS *completion callback*, not a timer or an await.
  Call it out in the README and the video.
- **Show the JSON swap.** In the video or README, change `quiz.json` to a
  5-option question and show it rendering unchanged. That proves "dynamic".
- **Prove performance, don't claim it.** Include the DevTools screenshots and one
  sentence: "wrong answers rebuild only the quiz card."
- **Tests.** `flutter test` passing on `QuizModel` shows engineering maturity few
  interns include.
- **Accessibility.** Mention the 60px tap targets and `Semantics` on options.
- **Polish the README top.** A GIF + a one-line pitch beats three paragraphs.
- **Honest AI section.** The rejected-suggestion story (optimistic `completed`)
  shows judgment, which is what they're really testing.

## 6. Senior Flutter Staff Engineer Review

What I'd say in a code review, and what would push this from "good intern" to
"shortlist immediately":

**Strengths**
- Genuine separation of concerns: services are pure Dart and unit-testable;
  widgets only compose and derive.
- The two state machines (`AudioState`, `AnswerStatus`) make the UI a pure
  function of state — easy to reason about and extend.
- Performance is designed-in (RepaintBoundary, `.select()`, single ticker,
  vector art), not bolted on.
- Error paths are real, not decorative — typed exceptions, retry, no crashes.

**Improvements that raise the odds**
1. **Add a couple of widget tests** with provider overrides: force
   `AudioState.completed` and assert the quiz appears; force `error` and assert
   the retry card. This directly demonstrates the gate works.
2. **Extract a `BuddyState` derivation test** — it's a pure function, perfect for
   a quick unit test.
3. **Cancel safety:** if the user backgrounds the app mid-narration, call
   `stop()` in a lifecycle observer so a stale completion can't fire.
4. **Content scalability:** make `quizProvider` take a question id / list so the
   path to "multiple questions" is obvious — even if you ship one now.
5. **Theming:** move text styles into the `TextTheme` so widgets reference
   `Theme.of(context).textTheme` rather than inline `TextStyle`s. Cleaner and
   more "production".
6. **l10n:** strings are centralised; wiring `flutter_localizations` would be a
   strong "thinking ahead" signal for a kids' product shipping globally.

None of these are blockers — the submission is already strong. Items (1) and (2)
are the highest-leverage additions before you submit.
